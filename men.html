NUTRITION

In nutrition, diet is the sum of food consumed by a person or other organism.
The word diet often implies the use of specific intake of nutrition for health or weight-
management reasons (with the two often being related). Although humans are
omnivores, each culture and each person holds some food preferences or some
food taboos. This may be due to personal tastes or ethical reasons.

  • Nutrition needs differ with gender and age.
  • As a man, your healthy diet will need to meet your unique physical needs,
    fit with your lifestyle, and reduce your risk of disease.
  • A healthy diet for a man is different from a healthy diet for a woman.
    And what a middle aged male needs from his food for good health and growth
    differs from what a male baby, child, teenager or older
    person needs.

We can categorize men's nutrition according to their age. Let's see them:

19 to 50 years

  • Younger men in this age bracket generally need more energy than older men,
    but still need to eat in moderation, with a good variety and balance
  • There’s about 60 grams of protein in 100 grams of rump steak and
    12.4 grams of protein in 40 grams of reduced fat cheese.
  • So it’s not much – and it’s easy to achieve if you’re eating protein-rich
    foods like meat, poultry, fish, dairy, legumes and lentils, eggs and nuts.
  • To manage your weight, you’ll need to limit high kilojoule drinks, such as soft drinks,
    energy drinks and alcohol.

50 to 71 years

  • By this age, your nutritional needs have changed from when you were younger.
    As men get older, the key is choosing foods that give you bang for your buck – that is,
    foods that are nutrient-rich, without excessive kilojoules.
  • You need slightly less of some food groups, such as lean meats and eggs, tofu, nuts and seeds.

71 + years

  • At this age, you need slightly fewer grain (cereal) foods and slightly more dairy foods and their
    alternatives, such as milk, cheese, yoghurt and calcium-enriched rice milk.

PHYSICAL HEALTH

Physical health is an important aspect of overall well-being for men, just as it is for women.
Taking care of one's physical health involves making healthy choices and engaging in regular exercise,
maintaining a balanced diet, getting enough sleep, and avoiding risky behaviors such as smoking,
excessive alcohol consumption, and drug use.

Here are some specific aspects of physical health that are important for men to consider:

Prostate health

  • As men age, the risk of prostate problems such as an
    enlarged prostate or prostate cancer increases.
  • Regular check-ups and screenings can help detect problems early
    and increase the chances of successful treatment.

Testosterone

  • Testosterone levels naturally decline with age, which can lead
    to decreased energy, decreased muscle mass, and changes in mood and libido.
  • If you're experiencing symptoms related to low testosterone,
    talk to your doctor about testosterone replacement therapy.

Heart health

  • Men are at a higher risk for heart disease than women, so it's important
    for them to take steps to protect their heart health,such as maintaining
    a healthy diet, exercising regularly, and avoiding smoking and excessive
    alcohol consumption.

Sexual health

  • Men should take care of their sexual health by practicing safe sex
    and getting regular check-ups and screenings for sexually transmitted
    infections.

Musculoskeletal health

  • Regular exercise and stretching can help prevent injury and maintain
    strong bones, muscles, and joints.

Remember, mental health is just as important as physical health and seeking help
is a sign of strength, not weakness.

MENTAL HEALTH

Mental health refers to a person's emotional, psychological, and social well-being.
It affects how we think, feel, and behave in our daily lives. Good mental health allows
us to cope with the normal stresses of life, work productively, and make meaningful contributions
to our communities.
Unfortunately, many people experience mental health challenges at some point in their lives,
such as anxiety,depression, bipolar disorder, schizophrenia, or eating disorders. It's important to seek help
if you or someone
you know is struggling with mental health issues.

Here are some steps you can take to support your mental health:

Practice self-care

  • Take care of your physical health by eating well, sleeping enough, and exercising regularly.

Connect with others

  • Spend time with loved ones and participate in social activities that bring you joy.

Manage stress

  • Find healthy ways to cope with stress, such as meditation, yoga, or exercise.

Seek professional help

  • If you are experiencing mental health challenges, don't hesitate
    to reach out to a mental health professional for support.
